01About

Hardware that earns its weight.

A mechanical engineering student at UMass Amherst with internships at Raytheon and Smith & Wesson. My work sits at the intersection of design, manufacturing, and test.

My passion for engineering started young — building model rockets, taking apart small engines, and asking too many questions about how things flew. That curiosity pointed me toward aerospace, and specifically toward military aeronautics, unmanned aerial vehicles, and propulsion. It's still where I want to spend my career.

I transferred from Springfield Technical Community College, where I helped launch the Mechanical Engineering Club, before continuing at UMass. Along the way I've designed test fixtures for firearm durability programs at Smith & Wesson, built 3D-printed shop aids and shadow-boards for missile production at Raytheon's Tucson site, and led a senior capstone on two-axis thrust vector control for active rocket stabilization.

Off the clock I lift, run, and spend time in my family's machine shop — manual mills, lathes, drill presses — keeping the practical side of fabrication sharp.

PROJ · TVC-01 SENIOR CAPSTONE · 2026

Thrust Vector Control Rocket Stabilization System

UMass Amherst  //  Sponsored by Smith & Wesson

Led the development of a two-axis TVC system for active rocket stabilization. Designed and integrated a lightweight gimbal capable of ±20° articulation for real-time directional thrust control, then ran structural analysis and iterative optimization to cut weight while holding rigidity. Built and tested the embedded control stack — servo response, structural durability, and stabilization under dynamic conditions — and presented final performance at the capstone showcase.
