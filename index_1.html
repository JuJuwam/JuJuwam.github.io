01About

Hardware that earns its weight.

A mechanical engineering student at UMass Amherst with internships at Raytheon and Smith & Wesson. My work sits at the intersection of design, manufacturing, and test.

My passion for engineering started young, building model rockets, taking apart small engines, and asking too many questions about how things flew. That curiosity pointed me toward aerospace, and specifically toward military aeronautics, unmanned aerial vehicles, and propulsion. It's still where I want to spend my career.

I transferred from Springfield Technical Community College, where I founded the Mechanical Engineering Club, before continuing at UMass Amherst for a 4+1 MS with a graduate certificate in Engineering Project Management and Leadership. Along the way I've designed test fixtures and assembly-line tooling for firearm programs at Smith & Wesson, spent two summers as a manufacturing engineering intern at Raytheon, in Tucson on missile production shop aids and in Andover on chemical-mixing fixtures and additive manufacturing, and led a senior capstone on two-axis thrust vector control for active rocket stabilization. I'm a founding member of the UMass Liquid Propulsion team, where I led nozzle design and simulation for a 200 lbf engine concept.

Off the clock I hike, research military technological history, and spend time in my family's machine shop, manual mills, lathes, drill presses, keeping the practical side of fabrication sharp.

Thrust Vector Control Rocket Stabilization System

UMass Amherst  //  Sponsored by Smith & Wesson  //  4-person team lead

Led a 4-person capstone team developing a two-axis TVC system for active rocket stabilization. Designed a closed-loop PID control architecture integrating IMU feedback and servo actuation, with a lightweight gimbal capable of ±20° articulation for real-time directional thrust control. Ran structural analysis and iterative optimization to cut weight 54.8% while holding rigidity, then coordinated system assembly, procurement, additive manufacturing, and test campaigns to deliver a flight-ready prototype with 21 ms response time.
